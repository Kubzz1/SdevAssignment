Ireland's Ancient East is a remarkable region that invites tourists to delve into over 5,000 years of European history. This tranquil land is not only known for its green hills, charming towns, and calm waters but also for being steeped in history that ranges from the Celtic times to the modern age.


Here, you can have an intimate experience with history, like stepping inside a monument that has withstood millennia or enjoying the view from atop a 13th-century lighthouse. Places like the Rock of Cashel in County Tipperary offer a glimpse into Ireland's majestic past.


The journey through Ireland's Ancient East isn't just about visiting places; it's about the stories that have shaped Ireland. You can ride the waves to Spike Island in Cork, walk through the medieval streets of Waterford, or ponder the ancient myths atop the Hill of Tara in Meath.
